Networked Control Systems

Introduction

Networked control systems integrate sensors, controllers, actuators, computing devices, and physical processes through communication networks. They provide an important technological foundation for autonomous systems, smart manufacturing, connected vehicles, robotic networks, and cyber-physical infrastructure.

Communication networks improve system flexibility and enable distributed operation, but they also introduce practical challenges such as communication delays, packet loss, bandwidth limitations, asynchronous information exchange, quantization, and cyber-security risks. These factors can significantly affect system stability, safety, and control performance.

This project investigates theoretical, computational, and experimental methods for analyzing and controlling networked systems under realistic communication and operational constraints. The research combines control theory, optimization, graph theory, machine learning, numerical simulation, and experimental validation.
